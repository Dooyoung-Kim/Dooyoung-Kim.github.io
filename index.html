About Me

I received my PhD from the KAIST UVR Lab, researching AR/VR remote collaboration and spatial computing technologies. I have broad expertise in AR, VR, spatial computing, and Human-Computer Interaction (HCI), with deep research focus on AR/VR telepresence, mutual space generation, spatial experience sharing, and VR locomotion (Redirected Walking).

I am proficient in prototyping using HMDs (Hololens 2, Quest Pro, Vive Pro Eye, Quest 3, Vision Pro), primarily using Unity and Python as development tools. Currently, I am participating in the Meta-Museum project in collaboration with NYU, a system for sharing transcending spatial and temporal remote experiences using large media walls and MR HMDs. Additionally, I am leading the development of core XR technologies such as 3D modeling, interaction, hand tracking, and multimodal feedback, serving as a leader in Korean government-funded research projects.
